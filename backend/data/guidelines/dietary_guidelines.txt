HPB (Health Promotion Board) Dietary Guidelines — Singapore
Adapted for Baymax AI Health Companion (Synthetic Summary)

== MY HEALTHY PLATE ==

The HPB My Healthy Plate model recommends:
- Fill half your plate with fruits and vegetables.
- Fill one quarter with whole grains and high-fibre carbohydrates (brown rice, wholemeal bread, oats).
- Fill one quarter with lean proteins (fish, skinless chicken, tofu, legumes, eggs).
- Use healthier cooking oils in small amounts (canola, sunflower, olive oil).
- Drink water or unsweetened beverages throughout the day.

== CARBOHYDRATE CHOICES ==

Choose lower glycaemic index (GI) carbohydrates that release energy more slowly:
- Brown rice instead of white rice
- Wholemeal bread instead of white bread
- Oats and barley
- Sweet potato (in moderate portions)
- Most non-starchy vegetables

Limit high-GI carbohydrates:
- White rice, white bread, white noodles
- Sticky rice (glutinous rice) — very high GI, limit for diabetic patients
- Sugary cakes, biscuits, and pastries

Portion guidance for carbohydrates per meal:
- Rice: 1 to 2 cups (cooked) for most adults; 1 cup or less for those managing blood sugar
- Noodles: 1 to 1.5 cups cooked

== PROTEIN CHOICES ==

Preferred proteins (lower in saturated fat):
- Fish: aim for 2 to 3 servings per week, especially oily fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)
- Tofu and tempeh: excellent plant-based protein
- Skinless chicken breast or thigh
- Eggs: up to 7 per week for most adults (discuss with doctor if you have high cholesterol)
- Legumes: lentils, chickpeas, kidney beans

Limit:
- Red meat (beef, pork, lamb): no more than 3 servings per week; choose lean cuts
- Processed meats (sausage, luncheon meat, bacon): limit to occasional consumption due to high sodium and saturated fat

== VEGETABLES AND FRUITS ==

Aim for at least 2 servings of vegetables and 2 servings of fruit per day.
1 serving of vegetables = 1 cup raw leafy vegetables or half a cup cooked.
1 serving of fruit = 1 small apple, 1 small orange, or 10 grapes.

For diabetic patients: fruit is still recommended but limit to 2 servings per day and choose whole fruit over fruit juice.
High-fibre vegetables are especially beneficial: leafy greens (spinach, kailan, xiao bai cai), broccoli, cauliflower, cucumber.

== FATS AND OILS ==

Choose healthier unsaturated fats over saturated and trans fats.
Limit coconut milk, coconut cream, butter, lard, and palm oil (common in Singaporean cooking).
When stir-frying, use a small amount of oil (1 teaspoon per serving).
Avoid deep-fried foods as the main cooking method.

== HYDRATION ==

Drink 8 glasses (2 litres) of plain water daily.
Avoid sugary drinks: regular kopi/teh with sugar, teh tarik, milo, soft drinks, fruit juices, energy drinks.
Acceptable alternatives: water, plain tea without sugar, black coffee without sugar, unsweetened soy milk.
Sugar-free or diet drinks are not recommended as a replacement — plain water is always best.

== SODIUM AND SALT ==

Limit sodium intake to less than 2000mg per day (about 1 teaspoon of salt).
Be aware of hidden sodium in sauces: soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sambal are high in sodium.
Choose lower-sodium soy sauce where available.
When eating out, request less sauce and less salt in cooking.

== FOOD LABELS ==

Look for the Healthier Choice Symbol on packaged foods — these products have reduced sugar, sodium, or saturated fat.
Check nutrition labels per 100g: lower sodium (less than 400mg per 100g) and less than 10g sugar per 100g are better choices.

== MEAL TIMING AND FREQUENCY ==

Eat 3 regular meals per day. Do not skip meals, especially breakfast.
Avoid large meals — they spike blood sugar more than smaller, frequent meals.
Limit late-night snacking, which is associated with weight gain.
If hungry between meals, choose healthy snacks: a small handful of unsalted nuts, a piece of fruit, or plain crackers.
