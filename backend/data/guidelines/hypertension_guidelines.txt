MOH Clinical Practice Guidelines — Hypertension (Singapore)

== BLOOD PRESSURE TARGETS ==

For most adults with hypertension: target below 140/90 mmHg.
For patients with diabetes and hypertension: target below 130/80 mmHg.
For elderly patients aged 80 and above: a less stringent target of below 150/90 mmHg may be appropriate to reduce the risk of falls from low blood pressure.

== BLOOD PRESSURE MONITORING ==

Home blood pressure monitoring is encouraged for all patients with hypertension.
Measure blood pressure at the same time each day, preferably in the morning before taking medication.
Sit quietly for 5 minutes before measuring. Rest your arm at heart level.
Record both systolic (top number) and diastolic (bottom number) readings.
Two readings should be taken 1 to 2 minutes apart; record the average.
A consistent reading above 135/85 mmHg at home should be reported to your doctor.

== AMLODIPINE GUIDANCE ==

Amlodipine is a calcium channel blocker commonly used for hypertension. It is typically taken once daily.
It may cause ankle swelling (oedema) in some patients — this should be reported to the doctor but is usually not dangerous.
Amlodipine should be taken at the same time each day. If a dose is missed, take it as soon as remembered, unless it is almost time for the next dose.
Do not stop taking amlodipine without consulting a doctor, as blood pressure may rise quickly.

== LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS ==

Reduce salt intake: aim for less than 5g (one teaspoon) of salt per day total, including in cooking and from sauces.
Avoid adding soy sauce, fish sauce, or MSG liberally. Choose low-sodium versions where available.
The DASH diet (rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy, lean proteins) has been shown to lower blood pressure.
Limit alcohol: no more than 1 standard drink per day for women, 2 for men.
Quit smoking: smoking raises blood pressure and increases cardiovascular risk significantly.
Achieve and maintain a healthy weight: even a 5 to 10 kg weight loss can lower blood pressure meaningfully.

== EXERCISE RECOMMENDATIONS ==

Aim for at least 150 minutes per week of moderate aerobic exercise such as brisk walking or swimming.
Avoid isometric exercises (such as very heavy weightlifting) which can spike blood pressure.
Exercise in the cooler parts of the day; avoid exercising in extreme heat.
Stop exercising and rest if you feel chest tightness, breathlessness, or dizziness, and seek medical attention if symptoms persist.

== SALT RESTRICTION IN COMMON SINGAPOREAN FOODS ==

Hawker food is typically high in sodium. To reduce salt:
- Request less gravy or sauce for dishes like chicken rice, wonton noodles, and laksa.
- Choose porridge or steamed dishes over heavily seasoned or fried options.
- Avoid processed meats such as luncheon meat and hot dogs which are high in sodium.

== WHEN TO SEEK URGENT CARE ==

Call 995 or go to A&E immediately if:
- Blood pressure is above 180/120 mmHg and you have symptoms such as severe headache, chest pain, vision changes, or difficulty breathing (hypertensive emergency).
- Sudden severe headache, weakness or numbness on one side of the body, difficulty speaking, or confusion (possible stroke).

== REGULAR REVIEWS ==

Blood pressure check: every 1 to 3 months until at target, then every 6 months when stable.
Kidney function tests (creatinine, eGFR): annually.
Electrocardiogram (ECG): as directed by doctor.
