MOH Clinical Practice Guidelines — Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (Singapore)

== GLUCOSE TARGETS ==

For most adults with Type 2 diabetes, the recommended HbA1c target is below 7.0% (53 mmol/mol).
For older patients (70+) or those with multiple comorbidities, a less stringent target of below 8.0% (64 mmol/mol) may be appropriate to minimise hypoglycaemia risk.

Fasting blood glucose target: 4.0 to 7.0 mmol/L
Postprandial (2 hours after meals) blood glucose target: below 10.0 mmol/L

== METFORMIN GUIDANCE ==

Metformin remains the first-line oral medication for most patients with Type 2 diabetes who have no contraindications.
It should be taken with meals to reduce gastrointestinal side effects such as nausea or diarrhoea.
Patients should not stop or adjust their metformin dose without speaking to their doctor.
If a patient misses a dose, they should take it as soon as they remember unless it is close to the time of the next dose — in that case, skip the missed dose and resume the normal schedule.

== BLOOD GLUCOSE MONITORING ==

Patients on insulin should check blood glucose at least twice daily: fasting in the morning and before bedtime.
Patients on oral medications alone may monitor less frequently as directed by their doctor.
If blood glucose is below 4.0 mmol/L (hypoglycaemia), the patient should consume 15g of fast-acting carbohydrate (e.g., 3 glucose tablets, half a cup of fruit juice) and recheck after 15 minutes.
If blood glucose is above 15.0 mmol/L on two consecutive readings, the patient should contact their doctor or go to a clinic.

== DIETARY ADVICE FOR DIABETES ==

Choose carbohydrates with a lower glycaemic index (GI): brown rice, wholemeal bread, oats, and most vegetables.
Avoid sugary drinks such as kopi with sugar, teh tarik, soft drinks, and fruit juices.
Plate method: fill half the plate with non-starchy vegetables, one quarter with lean protein (fish, tofu, chicken without skin), and one quarter with carbohydrates.
Limit rice to no more than one cup (cooked) per meal.
Eat regular meals — do not skip meals, especially if taking diabetes medication, as this can cause low blood sugar.
Choose steamed, baked, or boiled cooking methods over fried options.

== EXERCISE AND LIFESTYLE ==

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity activity per week, such as brisk walking.
Break up long periods of sitting every 30 minutes with light activity.
Regular exercise helps lower blood glucose and improve insulin sensitivity.
Always carry a snack or glucose tablets when exercising to treat potential hypoglycaemia.

== HYPOGLYCAEMIA (LOW BLOOD SUGAR) ==

Symptoms: shaking, sweating, feeling dizzy, hunger, pale skin, confusion.
Treatment: take 15g fast-acting carbohydrate immediately, wait 15 minutes, retest.
Severe hypoglycaemia (unable to swallow or unconscious): call 995 immediately.

== FOOT CARE ==

Check feet daily for cuts, blisters, redness, or swelling.
Wear well-fitting shoes and never walk barefoot.
Any foot wound that does not heal within a few days should be reviewed by a doctor promptly.

== REGULAR SCREENINGS ==

HbA1c: every 3 months if not at target; every 6 months if stable.
Kidney function (urine albumin-creatinine ratio): annually.
Eye examination for diabetic retinopathy: annually.
Foot examination by healthcare professional: annually.
